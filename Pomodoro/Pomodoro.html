Q: Why the name "Pomodoro Timer"?
A: The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. It uses a timer to break down work into intervals traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. These intervals are known as "pomodori", the plural of the Italian word pomodoro meaning "tomato". The technique is named after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used when he was a university student.
